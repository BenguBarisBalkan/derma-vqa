Visual Question Answering in Dermatology

Dermatological diseases pose a significant global health challenge, often hindered by a shortage of specialists and limited access to immediate medical consultations. To address these issues, we propose an interactive web-based dermatological assistant integrating advanced AI technologies.

Our system allows users to upload images and enter free-text queries via a Flask-based interface. The uploaded images are processed using a Vision Transformer (ViT-Base) model fine-tuned on a 30-class dermatology dataset, achieving approximately 91% accuracy in classification.

After classification, our system uses a Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) module to retrieve relevant contextual information. The user's queries are reformulated into medical language using an advanced Large Language Model (LLM), specifically MiniLM-L6-v2, and compared against a database of 50 question-answer pairs per disease class generated by GPT-3o.

The system then generates medically accurate and empathetic responses to the user's queries, displayed interactively in the chat interface. Early evaluations indicate robust performance of our classification model and improved contextual relevance in responses from the RAG module.

Future plans include expanding support for multi-label classification, enhancing dialogue capabilities, integrating larger datasets, and conducting extensive clinical validation studies.

This tool aims to provide scalable, reliable, and interpretable preliminary dermatological consultations, particularly benefiting regions with limited access to specialists, thereby reducing diagnostic delays and empowering informed patient decisions about dermatological health.
